# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free experience for everyone participating in this project, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, experience level, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Be respectful and constructive in communication.
- Assume good intent and ask clarifying questions before escalating.
- Provide feedback focused on ideas, code, and outcomes, not people.
- Welcome newcomers and support collaborative learning.
- Respect differences in background, identity, and technical perspective.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, and other communication channels:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discriminatory language.
- Personal attacks, insults, or trolling.
- Sexualized language or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Threats, doxxing, or publication of private information.
- Deliberate disruption, spam, or bad-faith participation.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project-managed spaces and to public interactions that can reasonably be linked to this project and its community.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the maintainers by opening a private communication channel where possible (for example, repository security contact or maintainer email).

When reporting, include:

- What happened and where.
- Links, screenshots, or logs if available.
- Any immediate safety concerns.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled as confidentially as practical.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they consider appropriate, including warning, temporary suspension, or permanent ban from project spaces for behavior that violates this Code of Conduct.

## Good-Faith Expectations

Project maintainers will strive to:

- Evaluate reports fairly and consistently.
- Prioritize safety and respect for impacted individuals.
- Document actions taken when appropriate.
